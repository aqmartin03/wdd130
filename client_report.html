Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I decided that I wanted my client website to be about golf because it's one of my favorite sports and hobbies. I used AI to create images of the golf clubs and the golf course so that I wouldn't use a real life golf course.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

I communicated with my developer over Microsoft Teams. Thankfully, there were no issues with this. Whenever I reached out, he would respond in a reasonable amount of time. He was very helpful.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

My developer did an excellent job at meeting my expectaitions for the golf site. His clear communication and desire to receive feedback helped the site become something that I had imagined.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

Next time I hire a team to do a project for me, I will make sure that everyone is "all in". I heard from my lead developer one of my junior developers, but I never heard from the other junior developer. Although everything on the site looks great. I would just make sure everyone has mutual understanding of my expectations for the project.

Lead Developer Report

How did you manage your development team and comunicate the tasks for your junior devs?

I managed my development team in a few ways. I managed the communication in Microsoft Teams and ensured everyone was doing well in regards to the website. I assigned the child pages to my junior developers with no issues. I checked in with my team at least twice each week to make sure things were running smoothly. Overall, I felt that I executed my role as lead developer in a high level.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

Communication with my client was effective and powerful. There were many times that I asked my client for clarification on certain portions of his site plan, and he expressed his intentions and desires for it, and he was always very helpful. I didn't experience any issues.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

I handled publishing my client's site by communicating with client consistently, and I sent him the website link when he asked for it. Publishing his site was not that complicated.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

My experience with managing the site with GitHub was difficult at first, but after some practice, it became easier. At first, I didn't know how to upload an entire folder because it won't let you just select it, but then I figured it out. I enjoyed learning GitHub and I'm excited to continue using it in the future.

Junior Developer Report

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?

I communicated with both of my team leads through Microsoft Teams in our group chats. Communication was difficult with both of them because it seemed like they wouldn't respond very often. I knew one of them was really busy with work and was making the effort, while the other one would respond one time each week (on Saturday). That was frustrating, but I made the best with what I had.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?

For one of the sites, I had a general ideas of what I was doing. I had the images and I knew what child page I was responsible for, but I didn't have the wireframe until this past Saturday evening. As for the other site, I had no clue what I was doing because the client's site plan was so complex and long. I didn't know which child page I was responsible for until this past Saturday. After many hours and questions, I was able to complete these sites. I do appreciate the clients and lead developers for the work they did, no matter the amount of time it took.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?

I had heavy contributions to both of my sites for when I was a junior developer. As I said before, it took a while to understand my responsibilities for both sites, but once I did, I contributed heavily to the final product.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding your changes to the sites?

I didn't run into any issues with GitHub or with adding my changes. But before I added my code, I had to make sure that I didn't override code that others were using.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part of this project was in using CSS. The difficult thing about that is it consumed a lot of time. It seems to be more time consuming than HTML. I was able to overcome that challenge by never giving up, and by contiually seeking ways to improve each website's CSS.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

The thing I enjoyed most about this project is that I got to pick my own client website, and that I got to use my personal creativity to make my best version of each website. I genuinely enjoyed this project, and I would love to continue practicing the skills I have learned in this class.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

If I could do the project again, I would have started earlier in the week every week. This would have decreased the pressure of deadlines which I had to deal with. If I started earlier throughout each week, I may have been able to add some extra cool features.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

I feel more confident working on real-world projects because I have gained experience and practice in working with teammates, and in using CSS and HTML. Even if I work on other projects that don't use CSS and HTML, I would be confident because I know how to manage deadlines and expecations.
